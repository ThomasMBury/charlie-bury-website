<div class="poetry-content"> 

I thought I saw the Virgin in a tree.<br>
Perched halfway up in prayer and tunica,<br>
Head bowed, she balanced on the thick stem of a branch<br>
Where the verdure was ample and lush.

Beneath her mantle was a woad-blue, not quite brown,<br>
Her face resting in light shadow.<br>
The tree at the end of the vast meadow stood totally still,<br>
Stark and skeletal in late May.

A field of daffodils lay between us,<br>
And beyond, the deep furrows of barley.<br>
I looked again and still I could not make out the tree,<br>
But only the Virgin, half-standing peacefully.

What did this mean, by nature's grace?<br>
The purity of nature's healing made evident.<br>
I gripped the rosary around my neck:<br>
Qui ad te confugimus.

Through the meadow and up into the branches,<br>
Your statue is but an apparition –<br>
It passes the moment I move.

<div class="poetry-content"> 

The night does not resist the end,<br>
It welcomes darkness as a friend.<br>
The truth creeps in beneath the door,<br>
A light we could not see before.<br>
The mists of chaos clear away,<br>
The dust that settled in the day;<br>
The mind no longer cast adrift<br>
Receives the silence as a gift.

It finds its rhythm once again<br>
Beyond the worry and the pain:<br>
Secure, deliberate, and deep,<br>
A slow peace found within the sleep.<br>
'Tis not in revelations grand,<br>
Nor lightning striking on the land,<br>
But in the quiet breaking through<br>
Of all the things we thought we knew.

The dark gives way, not all at once,<br>
But in a slow and weary trance;<br>
Just as the tide retreats from land,<br>
To leave the smooth and washed-out sand,<br>
So night recedes and leaves the shore,<br>
More truthful than it was before.
